The History of Mexico

Mexico's background is marked by rebellion, conquest, and ancient civilizations. Up until Hernán Cortés' Spanish conquistadors overthrew the Aztec Empire in 1521, the region was home to extensive indigenous cultures such as the Maya and Aztecs. This marked the beginning of more than three centuries of Spanish colonization. México gained its freedom from Spain in 1821 after a conflict led by individuals such as Miguel Hidalgo. The Mexican American War (1846–1848), which resulted in enormous territory losses, and the French Intervention, which momentarily established a monarchy. The Mexican Revolution (1910-1917) brought social and political change, with figures like Pancho Villa and Emiliano Zapata campaigning for land reform and workers' rights, resulting in the Mexican Constitution of 1917.
